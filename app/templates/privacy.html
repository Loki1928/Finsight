Privacy

This page explains what Finsight stores about you and how to remove it. Finsight is a personal tool currently in a small, invite-only testing phase.

What is stored

  • Your account: your name and email address, taken from your Google sign-in. Finsight never sees or stores your Google password.
  • Transactions: the entries parsed from any bank or card statement you upload, plus any transactions you add manually.
  • Uploads: the statement files you upload, kept so parsing can be re-run if needed.
  • Feedback: any messages you submit through the feedback form.

Where it is stored

All data lives in a managed Postgres database (Neon, hosted on AWS ap-southeast-1). It is not sold, shared, or sent to any third-party analytics service. Your financial data is never sent to any external AI service.

Who can see your data

Your data is tied to your user account and isolated from every other user. Other testers cannot see your transactions, uploads, or feedback. Only the app owner (Lokendra Sharma) can access the database directly, and only for debugging purposes.

Deleting your data

You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time from your account page. This removes your transactions, uploads, feedback, and account record immediately and cannot be undone.

Data breach notification

In the event of a serious data breach affecting your information, you will be notified by email within 72 hours.
